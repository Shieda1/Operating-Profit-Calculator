What is the operating profit?
the operating profit of a firm is it's total earnings from it's core business functions for a specific period of time without the deduction of interest, taxes and any earnings from other businesses that the firm has a part interest in. It is also called operating income.
How to calculate operating profit?
To calculate operating profit you should use the following formula:
operating profit = gross profit -operating expenses
where:
gross profit = revenue - cost of goods sold
example: A company sold $63,000 worth of products and it cost the company $11,000 to provide these products and it also pay $4,000 on rent and electricity, calculate the operating profit:
first we should calculate the gross profit:
gross profit = revenue - cost of goods sold
gross profit = $63,000 - $11,000
gross profit = $52,000
and now let's calculate the operating profit:
operating profit = gross profit - operating expensess
operating profit = $52,000 - $4,000
operating profit = $48,000
Why use operating profit?
the operating profit is used because it is a good indicator of a business's health because it removes any irrelevant factor from the calcualtion and only takes into account the expenses that are necessary to keep the business running.
